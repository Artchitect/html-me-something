Architectural Landmarks

FALLING WATER

Designed by America's most famous architect Frank Lloyd Wright in 1935. Located in southwest Pennsylvania's Laurel Highlands, and is approximately 90 minutes from downtown Pittsburgh. The house resides atop of a waterfall, and was utilized as a private residence and weekend home.

TURNING TORSO

A neo-futurist residential skyscraper in Sweden, and stands as the tallest building in Scandinavia. Located in Malmo, it is regarded as the first twisted skyscraper in the world.

THE ROYAL OPERA HOUSE

The Copenhagen Royal Opera House is the national opera house of Denmark. Located on the island of Holmen in Central Copenhagen along the shore of the harbour, the building is one of the most expensive opera houses ever built. At 2.5 billion DKK. Which equates to 300,717 £.

WALT DISNEY CONCERT HALL

The Walt Disney Concert Hall was designed by starchitect Frank Gehry, and is one of the most unusual buildings in the centre of Los Angeles.
